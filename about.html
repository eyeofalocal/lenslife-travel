Our Mission

At Lenslife Travel, we believe in the transformative power of travel. Our mission is to connect you with Kenya's breathtaking natural wonders and vibrant cultures through unforgettable safari experiences.

We aim to inspire a lifelong love for exploration and a deep respect for our planet while supporting local communities and conservation efforts in the Maasai Mara region.

Our Journey

2020

Humble Beginnings

Founded by Tom and Jimmie, two passionate travelers who fell in love with Maasai Mara during their first safari. What began as personal passion evolved into a vision for sharing this magical place with others.

2021

First Group Tours

Launched our first official group safaris, focusing on authentic cultural experiences and wildlife encounters with minimal environmental impact.

2022

Conservation Partnership

Established partnerships with local conservation organizations and began contributing 5% of all profits to wildlife protection initiatives.

2023

Award-Winning Service

Recognized as "Best Emerging Safari Company" by Kenya Tourism Board for our innovative approach to sustainable tourism.

2024

Expanding Horizons

Added new destinations including Amboseli and Samburu while maintaining our commitment to small-group, high-quality experiences.

Our Philosophy

We believe travel in Maasai Mara should be more than sightseeing - it should be an immersive, enlightening experience that creates lasting connections.

Community First

We work directly with Maasai communities, ensuring they benefit fairly from tourism through employment and community projects.

Wildlife Protection

Our safaris follow strict ethical guidelines to minimize disturbance to animals and their habitats.

Sustainable Practices

From eco-friendly camps to carbon offset programs, we prioritize environmental responsibility.

Education Focus

We educate guests about conservation challenges and how they can make a difference.

Our Commitment to Maasai Mara

We are dedicated to responsible tourism that benefits both wildlife and local communities:

  • All our safari vehicles are eco-modified to reduce emissions
  • We employ exclusively local Maasai guides and staff
  • 5% of all profits fund community education programs
  • Partnership with Mara Conservancy for anti-poaching efforts
  • Plastic-free operations in all our camps and tours
