Frequently Asked Questions

Kenya offers excellent wildlife viewing year-round, but the best times are:

  • July-October: Peak season for the Great Migration in Maasai Mara (best for wildebeest river crossings)
  • January-February: Great for birdwatching and avoiding crowds
  • June-October: Generally dry season with animals congregating around water sources
  • November-May: Green season with fewer tourists and excellent photography conditions

Essential items to pack include:

  • Clothing: Neutral-colored clothing (khaki, green, beige), light layers for cool mornings, warm jacket for evenings
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes and sandals
  • Gear: Binoculars, camera with zoom lens, spare batteries/memory cards
  • Health: Malaria prophylaxis, sunscreen, insect repellent, basic first aid kit
  • Documents: Passport, visa, travel insurance, vaccination certificates

Recommended vaccinations include:

  • Required: Yellow Fever (if coming from an endemic country)
  • Recommended: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Tetanus, Polio
  • Malaria: Prophylaxis is strongly recommended for all safari areas
  • COVID-19: Check current requirements before travel

Please consult your doctor or travel clinic at least 6-8 weeks before departure.

We offer a range of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets:

  • Luxury Lodges: 5-star facilities with premium amenities and services
  • Tented Camps: Comfortable safari tents with en-suite bathrooms (our most popular option)
  • Mid-range Lodges: Comfortable 3-4 star accommodations
  • Budget Camps: Basic but clean accommodations for budget-conscious travelers
  • Private Villas: Exclusive use properties for families or groups

All our selected properties have excellent locations for wildlife viewing.

We arrange all transportation for your safari:

  • Road Transfers: Customized 4x4 safari vehicles with pop-up roofs for optimal viewing
  • Domestic Flights: Scheduled or charter flights between major parks (saves time)
  • Private Vehicles: Dedicated vehicle and driver-guide throughout your safari
  • Shared Vehicles: More economical option sharing with other guests

All our vehicles are specially equipped for safari with charging ports, coolers, and professional guides.

Kenya is generally very safe for tourists, especially in safari areas. We recommend:

  • Following your guide's instructions at all times in wildlife areas
  • Keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings in cities
  • Using registered taxis or our arranged transfers in urban areas
  • Not walking alone at night in cities
  • Keeping copies of important documents separate from originals

Our team monitors local conditions and will advise you of any safety considerations.

Safari cuisine is surprisingly varied and high quality:

  • Most lodges offer international cuisine with local influences
  • Fresh ingredients are used whenever possible
  • Vegetarian/vegan options are available at all properties
  • Special dietary requirements can be accommodated with advance notice
  • Bush breakfasts and sundowner drinks are safari highlights
