Kenya's Wild Wonders

Discover Africa's most spectacular wildlife destinations

Explore Kenya's Diverse Ecosystems

From the vast savannahs of Maasai Mara to the flamingo-filled shores of Lake Nakuru, Kenya offers some of the most diverse and breathtaking wildlife experiences on earth. Each destination provides unique encounters with nature.

Most Popular

Maasai Mara National Reserve

Best July-October 5-6hrs from Nairobi Big Five Present

The crown jewel of African safaris, the Maasai Mara is world-famous for the Great Migration when millions of wildebeest and zebra cross the Mara River. Enjoy exceptional big cat sightings year-round in this breathtaking savannah landscape.

Key Features:

  • The Great Migration (July-Oct)
  • Highest lion density in Africa
  • Hot air balloon safaris
  • Maasai cultural experiences
Book Mara Safari

Amboseli National Park

Best June-October & Jan-Feb 4hrs from Nairobi Kilimanjaro Views

Famous for its large elephant herds and stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Amboseli offers some of Africa's most iconic safari scenery. The park's swamps create perfect conditions for wildlife viewing and photography.

Key Features:

  • 1,600+ elephants in population
  • Postcard-perfect Kilimanjaro views
  • Excellent birdwatching (400+ species)
  • Maasai cultural manyattas
Book Amboseli Safari

Samburu National Reserve

Best June-October 5-6hrs from Nairobi Unique Species

This rugged wilderness in Kenya's northern frontier is home to species found nowhere else in the country - the reticulated giraffe, Grevy's zebra, and beisa oryx. The Ewaso Ng'iro river attracts abundant wildlife in this arid landscape.

Key Features:

  • Special "Samburu Five" species
  • Authentic cultural experiences
  • Less crowded than southern parks
  • Stunning semi-arid landscapes
Book Samburu Safari

Lake Nakuru National Park

Year-Round 2.5hrs from Nairobi Birdwatcher's Paradise

Famous for its flamingo-lined shores (when water conditions are right), Lake Nakuru is a compact park packed with wildlife including both black and white rhinos. The park's varied habitats support over 450 bird species.

Key Features:

  • Prime rhino viewing destination
  • Millions of flamingos (seasonal)
  • Easy add-on to other destinations
  • Scenic viewpoints and waterfalls
Book Nakuru Safari

Lake Naivasha

Year-Round 1.5hrs from Nairobi Boat Safaris Available

A freshwater oasis in the Great Rift Valley, Lake Naivasha offers a different safari experience with boat trips to see hippos and birdlife, walking safaris on Crescent Island, and cycling tours in Hell's Gate National Park.

Key Features:

  • Boat safaris among hippos
  • Walking with giraffes on Crescent Island
  • Hell's Gate cycling adventures
  • Relaxing lakeside lodges
Book Naivasha Trip

Nairobi National Park

Year-Round 30min from City Center Urban Safari

The world's only wildlife park bordering a capital city, Nairobi National Park offers an incredible introduction to Kenyan wildlife with rhinos, lions, and giraffes against a backdrop of city skyscrapers.

Key Features:

  • Easy half-day safari option
  • Successful rhino breeding program
  • Combine with elephant orphanage
  • Perfect for short layovers
Book Nairobi Safari

Private Wildlife Conservancies

Year-Round Varies by Location Exclusive Experience

Kenya's private conservancies bordering major parks offer exclusive, low-density safari experiences with activities not permitted in national parks - night game drives, walking safaris, and off-road driving for optimal wildlife viewing.

Key Features:

  • Fewer vehicles at sightings
  • Night game drives
  • Walking safaris with Maasai guides
  • Supports local communities
